Career Direction 2

Career Direction 2 is a comprehensive career guidance program designed to help high school students:

  • make appropriate educational and career choices.
  • enhance their job-finding knowledge and skills through useful tools and strategies.
  • understand the importance of making informed choices regarding alcohol, drugs, and personal relationships.

Career Direction 2 can be tailored for 1 to 3 class periods. Students will be fully engaged as they work through a program that is all about them - their priorities, their personality, their interests, and their abilities! They will consider possible careers as related to their interests, find out how to research the careers further, examine the education and training required, and be asked to think about the costs for that education.

One module of the material focuses on job-getting skills such as effective resume and cover letter writing. There is also a section covering job interview strategies, with four short example videos the students evaluate. The workshop is motivating and can truly help students figure out their next steps.

Career Direction 2 is an appropriate fit in English, business, or in a Counselor's "Special Event." Certain sections can also be tailored to be presented during appropriate subjects, e.g. the "Drugs and Alcohol Education" section is a great fit with health and PE, but could also fit in science, chemistry, or biology. It is also an excellent topic in the few weeks prior to Prom or Homecoming.

More information is available at the Career Direction 2 Website.

Program Requirements:

  • Up to 40 students with an educator in attendance
  • Minimal equipment requirements
  • Each single module is designed for 45-minute

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: Is this just a recruiting push where the Guard is sold to my students?

No. Guard Representatives teach these workshops as a way of building trust and rapport with students and faculty while doing something of value for the community and the students. If we do something that has value to all the students in the classroom, then those that may be military inclined will naturally seek us out to ask questions at another time. We won't pressure your students.

Q: I don't really understand the big picture of the Guard, will that be explained?

If you wish, it can certainly be a footnote as the presentation wraps up. When storms, floods, and disaster occur, the National Guard is there to help. When there is no flood, we still need good people to learn to be good Guard members - ready to serve.
